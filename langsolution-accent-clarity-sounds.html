/θ/
The TH Sounds — Voiceless & Voiced
The most common sound error for non-native speakers in legal and healthcare settings
⚖️ Legal
🏥 Healthcare
⭐ High Priority
/θ/ — Voiceless TH
think · therapy · breath · three
/ð/ — Voiced TH
the · this · whether · other
🔬 Tongue Position
Tongue tip lightly between upper and lower front teeth. Air passes through. Vocal cords completely silent.
🎯 How to Produce It
Place the tip of your tongue lightly between your upper and lower front teeth. Push air through gently. Your throat should feel NO vibration.
✋ The Finger Test
Place two fingers on your throat. Say "the." You should feel NO vibration for /θ/. If you feel buzz — you are saying /d/ instead.
⚖️ Legal Context
"The court finds that the evidence supports the claim." Every "the" is a practice opportunity.
🏥 Healthcare Context
"The therapy requires three treatments." Practice this slowly until every /θ/ is clean.
/ð/
Same tongue position as /θ/ — but vocal cords vibrate. Place fingers on throat and feel the buzz.
📷 Generate image in ChatGPT and add here
🎯 How to Produce It
Same tongue position as /θ/ — between the teeth. But this time turn your voice ON. Your throat should vibrate like a gentle buzz.
✋ The Finger Test
Place two fingers on your throat. Say "this." You SHOULD feel vibration for /ð/. No buzz means you are producing the wrong sound.
⚖️ Legal Context
"Whether the defendant acted in good faith is the question." — "Whether" and "the" both use /ð/.
